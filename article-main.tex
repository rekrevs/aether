\begin{abstract}
We explore a speculative but internally consistent effective-field-theory framework that is
compatible with the hypothesis that observable relativistic physics is an emergent low-energy
description of a discrete substrate with a global update ordering. In addition to spacetime proximity in an emergent manifold $(M,g)$ we introduce
a pattern space $(S,d_\sigma)$, where $d_\sigma$ measures structural similarity of local
configurations. A weak, substrate-local coupling in $S$---``aether resonance''---can then
redistribute energy and information between near-by patterns in $S$, and this projects as
apparently superluminal transfer in $M$.

At the level of effective field theory we derive the framework from a total action in which
visible fields are coupled to an aether/khronon sector and to an $S$-mediator via a bilocal
selection operator $O_S$. We obtain covariant split conservation laws for the visible and
$S$-sectors, together with an exchange current $J^\nu_\sigma$. Consistency with the Bianchi
identity forces the gravitational coupling to be universal: the factor multiplying $T^S_{\mu\nu}$
in the Einstein equations is fixed to $\alpha \equiv 1$ whenever $J^\nu_\sigma\neq 0$, so all
apparent FTL behaviour is pushed into substrate locality rather than modified gravity.

Structural proximity is implemented by a mediator field $\chi(\sigma,T)$ that propagates
locally on $(S,d_\sigma)$ with finite speed $c_S$ and generates an exponentially decaying
kernel $K_\sigma \propto \exp[-d_\sigma/\lambda_\sigma]$. The selection operator $O_S$ is
constructed from higher-dimension, gauge- and diffeomorphism-invariant seed operators and
normalized to $[O_S]=4$. Its expectation value is driven to zero in homogeneous or thermal
states by a ``degeneracy dilution'' mechanism, but can approach order one in carefully
engineered, pattern-rich configurations. This explains why collider and everyday signatures
are suppressed while specialised mesoscopic platforms remain sensitive.

We derive a Landauer-type thermodynamic resource inequality that bounds any FTL bit rate
by entropy production and pump power, and prove a modified Lieb--Robinson bound in which
substrate-mediated effects appear as a soft, exponentially suppressed tail outside the usual
light cone. A categorical causality analysis shows that, given a global substrate time function,
these dynamics forbid antitelephone protocols and closed causal loops even in the presence of
S-local superluminal transfers. Finally, we map the parameters $(\varepsilon,\lambda_\sigma,Q)$
onto photon-sector coefficients of the Standard-Model Extension at the order-of-magnitude
level and outline three ``no-loophole'' experimental designs (E1--E3) that would either
significantly tighten Lorentz-violation bounds in this class of models or reveal a reproducible,
pattern-dependent superluminal channel.
\end{abstract}
