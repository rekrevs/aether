\begin{abstract}
We formulate a consistent, falsifiable hypothesis in which observable relativistic physics is an emergent low-energy description of a discrete substrate with global update ordering. In addition to spacetime proximity in the emergent manifold $(M)$, we assume \textbf{structural proximity} in a pattern space $(\Sspace)$ with a structural distance metric $\Dsig$ that measures algorithmic similarity. A \textbf{substrate-local} coupling in $(\Sspace)$ --- \textbf{aether resonance} --- can then transport energy and information between isomorphic substructures and project as FTL in $(M)$.

Starting from a total action $S_{\mathrm{tot}}$ that couples visible fields to an $S$-mediator and a pattern-selection operator $\Osel$, we derive the field equations, conservation laws, and consistency conditions. Key results include: (i) \textbf{action-level formulation} with well-defined $\TS$ and covariant conservation laws, (ii) a \textbf{split conservation law} for visible and $S$-sector stress-energy with exchange current $\Jsig$, (iii) an \textbf{explicit proof} that the Einstein equations require a \emph{universal} gravitational coupling $\alphag\equiv 1$ even in the presence of exchange, enforced by the Bianchi identity, (iv) \textbf{construction of a retarded $S$-mediator} that realizes locality and exponential falloff in $\Dsig$, (v) a \textbf{selection operator} $\Osel$ that renders the effect invisible in standard-model scattering, (vi) an \textbf{operationalized $\Dsig$-metric} with length units via a ``distance ladder'' relating microscopic pattern moves to macroscopic displacements in $(M)$, (vii) an \textbf{information-theoretic interpretation} of resonance as a ``similarity pump'' with entropy cost, (viii) a \textbf{thermodynamic resource inequality} that bounds FTL bitrate by entropy production, (ix) a \textbf{modified Lieb-Robinson bound} with explicit conditions, (x) a \textbf{formal causality argument} preserving the null-cone structure, (xi) an \textbf{anisotropy budget} and mapping to Standard Model Extension (SME) coefficients, and (xii) stringent, \textbf{no-loophole} experiments (E1--E3) with mapped parameter inference and multiple-test correction.
\end{abstract}
